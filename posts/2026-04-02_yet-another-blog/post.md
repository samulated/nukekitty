# Yet another blog

I find myself repeating myself often, not only in conversation but also in my actions.

I love to start projects. Start writing blogs and newsletters, start planning big and long builds or fixes, start committing to things and lock in. Unfortunately, I'm really bad at longevity, keeping my head in the game, and finishing.

And then, of course, I don't end up developing the skill because I'm not actually finishing it up myself and looking back on it and allowing it to be real.

I'm kind of sick of that.

## Making things real

In the world today, of 'AI' generation and fabricated events, it's getting increasingly more important to actually make things. I want to do things with real people, even if (through online spaces and especially the k-fabe of furry) we're not talking about Physical Reality. But I want to make friends with people who actually exist, build real relationships with them, make things with them, and come out a different person at the end.

Lets both alter the trajectory of eachothers lives for a moment.

Right now I'm really trying to focus on:
- Making things
- Spending specific 1-on-1 (or small group) time with people
- Leaning into old tech, indie tech, and nostalgia

I want there to be joy and intrigue and awe in the world. We have to lean towards the things that fire us up, keep us warm, and

But we make it real!

## If you can't join them, beat them

Megacorporations are pushing everyone out of what used to be nice, fun, collaborative spaces. The put up walls and clean designs and strip us of everything that makes it worth it. They are taking our spaces and carving them into their own false realities.

They pump them full of ads and algorithms to extract as much data from and make as much money on you as they can. Then they try to charge you for it.

They shut down 'adult' content because the payment providers don't like it, regardless of if that's the entire reason you're there in the first place

They shove features that no one wants into everything, breaking the core user loop completely.

And let's not get started on how hard _every_ platform has embraced modern fascism with open arms.

And so, while it's hard, we really have to lean into making our own stuff, having our own spaces, and banding together with our people. We can't join them, they are preying on us, they breaking us down and splitting us apart and charging us for it. We cannot live in their constructed reality, it will not sustain human life.

We have to make it real.

## Surviving the desert

As an ex-Twitter user I can tell you, it's not easy to divest yourself of these toxic platforms, especially when everyone you know and don't know are there. They become part of your routines, and how you communicate with people.

For me, it reached a point where, on an account reserved for adult activities (and whose posts and activity reflected this), I repeatedly received push notifications of posts from straight up actual white supremacists and supporters of (actual 1900s) Nazis, and even going and blocking their accounts didn't stop it from coming through.

That kind of shit is just way too much, and no amount of kinky twinks and furries can really make up for that.

But hey, if you move to a smaller space with less going on, that still has the things you like (just less of it) and also doesn't completely radiation blast you with hate every time you get a notification, you'd never guess. It actually feels a lot better. I feel a lot better.

It takes a while to get over not having 'the algorithm' hand feeding you attention-grabbing content. You're also going to feel left out on posts for those who still use the Other Place. I also feel a bit like That One Friend That's Too Woke every time I announce that I don't use Twitter anymore when someone who I've found mutual interest in throws me their X, the everything app (Formerly Twitter), username.

Babes, you're allowed to get out of the dumpster-fire.
